I guess being born in the digital era means when i was grounded as a kid that usually didnt include my toys,
those semi-forgotten analog plastics and fabrics that defied creativity and i would go to as a last resort.

The digital era also got rid of toy turism and even early forms of trading.
There was some sort of parallelism that existed when i had playdates and would see a friends setup in their own room.
As if the toy basket or chest or box or predetermined corner would mutate across different dimensional childhoods, all including their own type of action figure, vehicle and neglected baby toy.

Looking at your left out baby toys as a toddler was some sort of feeling, that barely resembles that of glancing at your toys when the ipad battery ran out.
