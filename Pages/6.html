Its an eerie feling, it really is.
Looking back on a childhood memory and analyzing the emotions that led your actions, as unaware of them as you were at that age.
Almost like dreams come with a sense of understanding that couldnt be had just from context clues, it is engrained in the altered consciousness;
Imagine reading a script before watching a movie that only has scenes for half of the story.

In that sense,
childhood life hides prejudice and morality and cooks only in the stove of discovery.
Everything was new and with discovery, the awe of it all leaves no space to judgement.
Judgement as the all dimensional scale that weights our experiences.

Nowadays everything is either a good outcome or a poor choice or a chance to improve or good luck, thumbs up this or thumbs down that.
The standard is so low “meh” became some sort of divine belief only the enlightened thinkers strive for.

“How was your day” and in silence i ramble through the parallax using my evolutionary social skills to determine i cant remember when we started doing this guilty/proud thing.

Kids have discovery, we adults have car keys.
