“And i turned out fine”.
Most often said when neglecting a child, this fallacy i dont care to analyze.
Im much more interested in this idea arranged in a hurry where, our mediocrity seems to us as the utmost pure example of human condition,
and therefore give it a stretched sense of value.

When asked if you would do anything differently in life theres this popular option where the answer is no: all of my mistakes have made me.
The existential man is so scared of losing this made up identity they wouldnt bargain with it, they wouldnt even touch it even if it put them out of mediocrity.

To parent your child better. Not better in a way where you dramatically avoid those things that make you not call your own parents sometimes.
Actually better.

Youve gotta accept you didnt turn out fine.
Because you dont exist. Be born everyday.
